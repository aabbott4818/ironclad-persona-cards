Unifying thread

Teaching narrative

One message, four entry points.

Accepted truth: legal is responsible for managing contract risk.

Reframe: the real question is not “how do we make legal faster?” but “how do we redesign the contract lifecycle so legal applies judgment only where it matters?”

New way: CLM with AI does not replace legal judgment; it removes repetitive work, improves visibility, and makes that judgment more scalable.

General Counsel

Strategic legal leader under volume pressure

Risk allocationDefensibilityBoard visibility
Person / world

Strategic advisor to the board who wants legal to be seen as a business enabler rather than a contract processing center.

Problem statement

Contract volume has outpaced team capacity, so senior legal attention is consumed by low-leverage work and the GC risks becoming reactive rather than strategic.

Commercial consequence

When review capacity cannot scale, risk handling becomes inconsistent, escalations rise, and the legal function looks operational instead of strategic.

Discovery prompts
Where does senior legal time get pulled into contracts that should not require senior attention?
Which contract issues create the most escalation, reputational concern, or executive visibility?
Where do you worry risk is being accepted without enough transparency or control?
Legal Operations

Workflow architect looking for measurable improvement

IntakeAutomationSLA
Person / world

Internal consultant and systems operator for the legal function, often acting as the mobilizer or champion for CLM change.

Problem statement

Manual intake, weak request visibility, inconsistent routing, and poor metadata make legal feel like a black box to the business.

Commercial consequence

If Legal Ops cannot prove cycle-time and workflow improvement, legal loses influence over enterprise systems decisions and adjacent teams fill the vacuum.

Discovery prompts
How do business stakeholders submit requests today, and what happens next?
Where does work most often stall between intake, approval, negotiation, and signature?
Which metrics do stakeholders ask for that are hardest to produce quickly?
Revenue leadership

Forecast owner who experiences legal as friction

Deal velocityRedlinesForecast risk
Person / world

CRO, VP Sales, or enterprise sales leader focused on pipeline progression, rep productivity, forecast accuracy, and closing confidence.

Problem statement

Deals stall in redlines, contract status is not visible enough, and legal friction appears late in the quarter when there is the least room to recover.

Commercial consequence

Contract delays consume selling time, erode forecast confidence, create end-of-quarter fire drills, and can delay revenue recognition.

Discovery prompts
What percentage of deals slip because contract negotiation takes longer than expected?
Where does legal friction show up most often in your sales cycle?
How visible is contract status today to frontline managers and forecast owners?
Finance leadership

Control-oriented buyer focused on exposure and auditability

ObligationsAudit trailExposure
Person / world

CFO or finance leader responsible for governance, compliance, obligations, liabilities, and the financial consequences embedded in contracts.

Problem statement

Critical obligations are buried in documents, renewals create avoidable exposure, and contract data is too slow to retrieve when leadership or auditors ask for it.

Commercial consequence

When obligations cannot be surfaced on demand, the business carries hidden compliance and financial risk while finance operates with incomplete visibility.

Discovery prompts
How long does it take to surface contract obligations for an audit or board request?
Where do renewals, commitments, or payment terms create unwanted exposure today?
Which contract data would finance most value in a structured and reportable format?
Learning loop

What to memorize this week

  • The CLM lifecycle in your own words: intake, approvals, redlining, execution, repository, reporting.
  • Twenty to thirty contract and workflow terms, each with a plain-English meaning and business consequence.
  • A 90-second version of the teaching narrative tailored to GC, Legal Ops, Revenue, and Finance.
  • Three discovery questions per persona that prove you understand workflow, risk, and economics.
  • Four plain-English clause summaries from two NDAs and one MSA.
